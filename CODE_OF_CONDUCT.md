# Code of Conduct

NMTH Climate Services is intended to support practical, reliable software for
cultural heritage operations. The project should be a respectful place for
maintainers, users, and contributors.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and constructive.
- Assume good intent, but be precise when discussing risks.
- Keep feedback focused on the work, not the person.
- Make room for contributors with different levels of operational, museum, or
  software engineering experience.
- Treat security, privacy, and production reliability concerns with care.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, insults, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing private information, secrets, internal hostnames, or operational
  data without permission.
- Derailing issues or pull requests with unrelated arguments.
- Pressuring maintainers to disclose sensitive deployment details.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments and may limit participation
when behavior harms the project or its users. Concerns can be raised through a
GitHub issue if they are safe to discuss publicly. Do not post private or
security-sensitive details in public issues.
